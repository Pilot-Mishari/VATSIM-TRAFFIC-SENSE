# SectorSense — Project Abstraction & Assumptions

## Project Overview

SectorSense is a web platform designed to analyze, monitor, and predict VATSIM traffic and controller workload.

The platform aims to help:

* Controllers
* vACC/FIR staff
* Event coordinators
* Mentors
* Pilots

make better operational decisions using live and historical traffic analysis.

---

# Problem Statement

VATSIM controllers and staff currently rely heavily on:

* manual observation,
* experience,
* Discord coordination,
* and guesswork

when determining:

* staffing requirements,
* sector openings,
* event preparation,
* and workload distribution.

There is currently no centralized system that:

* analyzes live and historical traffic,
* predicts controller workload,
* or provides intelligent staffing recommendations.

---

# Project Goal

Develop a scalable platform that:

* monitors live VATSIM traffic,
* analyzes traffic patterns,
* predicts future workload,
* and recommends staffing levels for airports and FIRs.

# Core Abstractions

## Traffic Units

Aircraft are abstracted into workload units.

Example weighting:

```txt
Arrival = 3 points
Departure = 2 points
Overflight = 1 point
```

Reason:
Different aircraft operations create different controller workloads.

---

## Sector Load

Sector workload is represented as:

```txt
sector_load = total traffic score within area
```

---

## Staffing State

Staffing recommendations are abstracted into levels:

```txt
LOW
MEDIUM
HIGH
VERY_HIGH
```

# Confirmed Assumptions

## 1. VATSIM data is publicly accessible and reliable

Status: Confirmed

The VATSIM data feed is stable enough for:

* live tracking,
* analytics,
* workload estimation,
* and historical analysis.

---

## 2. Traffic patterns repeat over time

Status: Confirmed

Examples include:

* weekend peaks,
* regional traffic trends,
* recurring event activity,
* and time-based traffic behavior.

This allows future prediction using historical datasets.

---

## 3. Aircraft count correlates with controller workload

Status: Confirmed

Higher aircraft count generally results in:

* more radio communication,
* increased sequencing,
* more coordination,
* and higher controller workload.

However, different aircraft operations should be weighted differently.

Example:

```txt
1 arrival > 1 overflight
```

because arrivals generate more workload.

---

## 4. Event schedules significantly affect traffic density

Status: Confirmed

Events are major traffic multipliers on VATSIM.

Examples:

* Cross the Pond
* Friday Night Ops
* Regional FIR events

Event activity should heavily influence prediction scoring.

---

## 5. ATC station availability increases traffic density

Status: Confirmed

Pilots actively choose destinations/routes based on ATC coverage.

Example behavior:

```txt
No ATC at airport:
Lower traffic

Approach online:
Traffic rises

Full staffing:
Traffic rises significantly
```

This creates a traffic feedback loop:

```txt
ATC online -> more pilots join -> more traffic
```

---

# Estimated ATC Influence Levels

## Delivery (DEL)

Low increase

Mainly affects:

* realism-focused pilots
* airline operation pilots

---

## Ground (GND)

Low increase

Improves airport operations but has lower global visibility.

---

## Tower (TWR)

Low increase

Important for arrivals/departures and airport activity.

---

## Approach (APP)

Moderate increase

Very influential because:

* pilots prefer controlled arrivals,
* sequencing improves realism,
* airports feel active.

---

## Center (CTR)

Moderate to high increase

Strong influence for:

* overflights,
* long-haul routes,
* FIR-wide visibility.

---

## 6. Long-duration ATC sessions increase traffic density

Status: Likely true

Pilots gain confidence when a station has remained online for a long period.

Reasons include:

* higher staffing stability,
* reduced risk of losing ATC mid-flight,
* increased confidence for long-haul operations.

Example:

```txt
APP online for 10 minutes:
small influence

APP online for 4 hours:
much larger influence
```
